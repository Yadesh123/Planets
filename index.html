What is a planet?

We might be fond of the planets Earth and Mars , but do we know WHY they are referred to as a planet when Pluto and Ceres aren't? Well, according to what the IAU said in 2006, planets should...

  • Orbit a star
  • Be big enough for enough gravity to force them into a sphere
  • Have a clean space around it

It was found that Pluto and some other "planets" didn't follow any of these! Do you think this list should be changed?

Mercury

This planet is the tiniest planet in the solar system- but as they say, it is the fastest! In fact, its name comes from the roman god Mercury because of its speed. It orbits the sun egg shapedly, with a speed of 47 kilometres per second. Each Mercury year is 88 days. It is the first planet, meaning the closest planet to the sun, having a distance of 0.4 Astronomical units (AU). Appearance wise, it is rocky, almost like the Earth, so it would be a terrestrial planet .

Venus

Venus is the second planet away from the sun. It is named after the roman goddess of love and beauty. Its mass makes it often be referred to as Earth's "twin". It is also (almost) Earth's twin in the sense that it orbits the sun every 224.7 Earth days. It, like antecedently mentioned Mercury , is a terrestrial planet, which as mentioned means rocky. It has the densest atmosphere of the four terrestrial planets.

Earth

This planet needs NO introduction! It is the planet we live in. This planet was formed over 4.5 billion years ago, and humans were said to occur in this vast planet in the first billion years. As you might already know, an Earth year is 365 days, thus, we can infer that the Earth takes 365 days to orbit the sun. It is the third planet from the sun. About 71% of its atmosphere is h2o and the other 29% is land. It is mostly composed of Nitrogen and Oxygen .

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun. It has a very thin atmosphere, made of mostly CO2, Nitrogen, Argon, and some oxygen/water vapour. A Mars day is more than 24 hours! You might be familiar with Mars quite a bit due to the countless amount of missions the world faced that were associated with this auspicious planet (ex. Perseverance and Curiosity )! Mars has two mooons, namely Phobos and Deimos . Fun fact : Although Mars may seem hot because of its red colour, it is actually extremely cold.

Jupiter

The fifth planet from the sun is the lovely Jupiter. This planet was also named after a roman god. That roman god is the KING OF ALL GODS! This planet, as funny as it sounds, is indeed a gas giant!. It is composed of mostly hydrogen. A special thing about the planet is that its moon count is abundant- it has eighty moons! But this number isn't even the largest in the solar system! (yet)

Saturn

One of the most talked about planets is indeed Saturn. This is because of its special ring! Its radius is nine and a half times the Earth's, and additionally, it is noted to be 95 times larger than the Earth. It is named after the god Saturn. It has 3 more moons than Jupiter (83 moons).
This planet is so auspicious that a day was made for it. The seventh day of the week Saturday is Saturn day (in Roman culture).

Uranus

The seventh planet away from the sun is Uranus! It is a fun name, but the planet deserves a lot of respect. It is named after the greek god Uranus, who is said to be the great grandfather of ares and the grandfather of the popular Zeus ! It was found in March 13, 1781 by William Herschel, who was also the founder of two of Saturn's moons. Uranus has many moons like Saturn and Jupiter. However, as this planet is far, it takes 84 years to orbit the sun. Woah!

Neptune

Finally, we have Neptune. This planet was discovered by three people: Johann Gelle, Urbain Le Verrier, and John Couch Adams in September the 23rd of 1846. It was named after the Roman god of the Sea and its symbol represents that god's trident. It is therefore the farthest away from the sun. It is the fourth largest planet in diameter. It is mostly composed of hydrogen and helium, like Saturn. It, along with Uranus, is an ICE GIANT planet.
